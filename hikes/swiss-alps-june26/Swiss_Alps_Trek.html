Central Swiss Alps Trek

A 3-day point-to-point hut-to-hut hike: panoramic ridges, four alpine lakes, and a glacier-facing finale — Bannalp → Engelberg → Melchsee-Frutt → Planplatten.

🗓️ Fri 26 – Sun 28 Jun 2026 🥾 3 days · ~41 km ⛰️ +2,200 m 🛏️ 2 nights booked
Walenpfad is OPEN — opened Sat 20 Jun 2026 (brunni.ch). Cable cars at both ends running. Re-check the webcam/weather the day before for snow up high.

Day 1 · Friday 26 June

Walenpfad — Bannalp → Engelberg

📏 14.8 km⬆️ +713 m⬇️ −833 m⏱️ ~5 h📈 T2
  1. 1Chrüzhütte 1718 mStart — top of the Bannalp cable car. Head toward Bannalpsee. Follow signs: Walenpfad / Brunni.
  2. 2UrnerstafelAlpine dairy farm near the lake where the ascent begins.
  3. 3Walegg 1951 mHigh point of the day — steep zig-zag climb, then descent the other side.
  4. 4Oberfeld 1884 mAlpine meadow. Alp Oberfeld Bergbeizli serves goat cheese, meat & yoghurt.
  5. 5Rigidalalp 1716 mMountain restaurant; the trail levels out and widens.
  6. 6Brunni / Ristis 1600 mEnd of hike → cable car down to Engelberg.
🚠 Descent: Engelberg–Ristis cable car Fri 26 Jun runs 08:00–17:00, every 20 min (last down ~17:00). Single descent CHF 20 (CHF 10 with Half-Fare). Tip: the Walen Trail “2-Bergbahnen” ticket — CHF 38 (CHF 28 Half-Fare) covers the Bannalp lift up and Ristis down, both used today.
⚠️ Before you go Start by ~08:00 to beat afternoon thunderstorms. Check brunni.ch/walenpfad webcam for snow on the Walegg high section.
Day 2 · Saturday 27 June

Four Lakes Walk — Engelberg → Melchsee-Frutt

📏 13.8 km⬆️ +775 m⬇️ −640 m⏱️ ~4.5 h📈 T2
✅ Status — open / operational Titlis Xpress to Trübsee plus the Trübsee–Jochpass & Engstlenalp–Jochpass chairlifts are all running (checked 20 Jun 2026); Four Lakes season runs from mid-June. Watch for a snow patch at the Jochpass.
Start Walk to the Titlis cable car in Engelberg and ride up to Trübsee to begin. Follow the green Vier-Seen-Wanderung (Route 574) stickers.
  1. 1Trübsee 1796 mStarting lake (Titlis mid-station). Walk the left shore.
  2. 2Jochpass 2207 mSteep climb — high point of the day. Chairlift shortcut from Trübsee if tired.
  3. 3Engstlensee 1850 mDeep-blue second lake on the far side of the pass.
  4. 4Engstlenalp 1834 mSmall settlement and hotel just past the lake.
  5. 5Tannalp 1976 mThird lake (Tannensee) and Berggasthaus Tannalp.
  6. 6Melchsee-Frutt 1920 mFlat path along Tannensee & Melchsee to the resort village. → Berghotel Distelboden.
⚠️ Snow patch The Jochpass (>2,200 m) can hold packed snow into late June — waterproof boots & poles help.
Day 3 · Sunday 28 June

Horizontweg — Melchsee-Frutt → Planplatten

📏 12.1 km⬆️ +715 m⬇️ −411 m⏱️ ~4 h📈 T2
✅ Status — open Meiringen-Hasliberg shows the Horizontweg open (accessible from end of June). Possible lingering snow on the Balmeregghorn ridge (2,255 m).
  1. 1Melchsee-Frutt 1920 mStart. Walk east across the plateau toward Tannalp (~45–60 min).
  2. 2Tannalp 1976 mThe climb begins — trail turns south and zig-zags up. Signs: Balmeregghorn / Planplatten / Horizontweg.
  3. 3ErzeggRidge station on the climb; follow the red-white-red markers.
  4. 4Balmeregghorn 2255 mRidge crossing & hardest climb. The Bernese Alps explode into view — Eiger, Mönch, Jungfrau.
  5. 5Planplatten / Alpen tower 2245 mTrail end. Hike is over here.
🚠 Descent to your train From Planplatten ride the gondolas down: Planplatten → Mägisalp → Hasliberg Reuti → Meiringen, then the train home. In the SBB app type Planplatten as the start.

Getting there & back from Basel

→ There · Day 1 morning (~2.5 h) SBB app: Basel SBB → Chrüzhütte (Bannalp). Basel → Luzern → Wolfenschiessen (train) → Oberrickenbach Talstation (PostBus 331) → Chrüzhütte (cable car). Hike starts at the top.
🚠 Bannalp cable car · Fell → Chrüzhütte (up to the start) 6 Jun–20 Sep 2026: every 30 min, 08:30–12:00 & 13:00–17:30 (plus a 17:50 run). First ride up 08:30. Operator tip: if your PostBus reaches the valley station at xx:04, take the xx:30 cable car.
← Back · Day 3 afternoon (~2.5–3 h) SBB app: Planplatten (or Meiringen) → Basel SBB, via Interlaken or Luzern.
🚠 Descent gondolas · Planplatten → Meiringen (Sun 28 Jun) Planplatten/Alpen tower → Mägisalp: daily 09:00–16:30 — last descent from Planplatten 16:30 (last up from Mägisalp 16:15). Then Mägisalp → Hasliberg Reuti runs to ~17:00, and the Reuti → Meiringen cableway drops you at the train. ⏰ Plan to reach Planplatten by ~16:00 so you don't miss the last ride down.
Driving instead?

Park at Stans (~1 h from Basel): 35-min train/bus to the Oberrickenbach cable car to start; on Day 3, Meiringen → Stans is ~1 h 15.

Food & provisions (trail lunches)

🛒 Engelberg — stock up for Day 2 (Fri evening) Coop (Titliszentrum 5, by the station, ~5 min from the Ski Lodge) and Migros (Klosterstrasse, centre). Tourist-resort hours incl. Sunday — roughly Mon–Sat ~08:00–18:30, Sun ~09:00–18:00 (verify on the day). Grab fruit, bread & cheese here the evening you arrive.
🛒 Melchsee-Frutt — small shop only (for Day 3) Just one little grocery on the plateau: Tutti Frutt / Frutt Laden (Frutt 2, +41 41 669 09 09), open during cable-car season — limited choice, can pre-order cheese/meat platters. Safer bet: buy Day-3 trail food already in Engelberg, and/or ask Berghotel Distelboden for a packed lunch (Lunchpaket) at dinner.

💸 How to pay less • Supersaver tickets (Sparbillette) in the SBB app — book your two travel days in advance for up to ~70% off; the biggest easy saving.
• Saver Day Pass — 1-day unlimited train+bus (also covers the PostBus); compare vs point-to-point on each long travel day.
• Half-Fare Card (Swiss Half Fare Card, ~CHF 120/1 month) — 50% off trains, buses & most cable cars (not the Bannalp line). Roughly break-even for this trip alone; worth it only if you travel more.
• Skip the "combo" mountain tickets here: the Walen Trail 2-Bergbahnen ticket (CHF 38) costs more than Bannalp + Ristis singles (CHF 33) because it bundles a chairlift you won't use.

Train fares are estimates — confirm in the SBB app. Mountain fares from operator sites, subject to change.
